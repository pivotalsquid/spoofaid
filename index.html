but spf and dkim are not enough,

DMARC
domain-based message authentication, reporting and conformance

allows a sender's domain to indicate that their emails are protected by SPF and/or DKIM, and tells a receiver what to do if neither of those authentication methods passes
yet another dns record
					 
					 	v=DMARC1;p=reject;pct=100;fo=1;
					 	rua=mailto:r30dy1g8@ag.dmarcian.com;
					 	ruf=mailto:r30dy1g8@fr.dmarcian.com;
dmarc works via alignment

alignment means that the domain in the from: field matches the domains in the spf and dkim
once dmarc is enabled, mail servers will generate reports detailing mail activity, allowing you to identify and track nefarious traffic
